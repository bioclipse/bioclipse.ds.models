LogP - Partition coefficient

Description

A measure of how hydrophilic ("water loving") or hydrophobic ("water fearing") a chemical substance is.

In organic chemistry and the pharmaceutical sciences, a partition- (P) or distribution coefficient (D) is the ratio of concentrations of a compound in the two phases of a mixture of two immiscible solvents at equilibrium, for example water and octanol.

Result values

  • XLogP - Prediction of logP based on the atom-type method called XLogP. Uses the Salicyl Correction factor implemented in CDK.
  • ALogP - Atomic based prediction of LogP according to Ghose-Crippen LogKow.
  • AMR - molar refractivity
